Our understanding

The dashboard works. It's been stalled.

Operations across San Francisco, London, and Tokyo depend on the inventory dashboard every day. The previous engagement ended with:

  • The Reports module half-finished
  • No automated tests, freezing IT change approvals
  • Documentation thin enough that future change carries unknown risk

The result: a working system that nobody is comfortable changing, and a list of capabilities — most importantly Restocking — that operations has been waiting on for months.

This isn't a rebuild. It's a stabilization-and-extension engagement.

Our approach

Stabilize first, then extend.

Sequencing principle

Every change lands behind a regression test. Every requirement closes on a demo to operations and a documentation update for IT.

Why this order

The test harness and architecture review come before any remediation, because they are what make the rest of the engagement safe to ship.

Engagement shape

  • 12 weeks, four phases
  • 3-person senior team, no offshore handoff
  • T&M with not-to-exceed cap, confirmed at the Week-2 discovery checkpoint
  • Bi-weekly status with hours, progress, and remaining cap headroom

Phase 3 · Weeks 6–9

Restocking capability

VP Operations' headline ask. The largest single piece of work in the engagement.

Backend

GET /api/restocking/recommend, accepting an operator budget, returning ranked recommendations with rationale. Greedy fill ordered by stockout risk — simple, explainable, tunable.

Frontend

New top-level view following established patterns: Composition API, scoped i18n keys from day one, api.js client. CSV export for downstream procurement use.

Discovery item: the codebase already calls /api/purchase-orders/* endpoints that don't exist on the server. We disposition this in Phase 1, then build cleanly.

Why us

Three reasons, briefly.

1

We've done this exact engagement before.

Our most recent comparable engagement was a 5-month vendor-handoff stabilization for a $22M logistics broker. Same shape — half-finished application, no test coverage, frozen change pipeline, paused operations features. Closed 23 logged defects, added a new operations view, retired the change freeze. Two more case studies in the written proposal.

2

Small and senior. No offshore handoff.

Three people, all with at least two years on the team, all hands-on. Tanaka's team and Meridian IT will speak to the same engineers from kickoff to closeout.

3

Honest scoping over heroic promises.

We've sized this engagement against an audit we haven't run yet. The Discovery Checkpoint at end of Week 2 is the moment we confirm the cap — based on the defect surface as it actually is. Meridian gets a real number, not a hopeful one.
