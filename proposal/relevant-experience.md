## Engagement 1 — Operational Dashboard Remediation, Halcyon Industrial Supply

**Client:** Halcyon Industrial Supply (B2B parts distributor, ~$22M revenue)
**Scope:** Inherited a Vue 2 / Flask dashboard from a prior vendor; audited and resolved 14 defects across three modules; migrated core views to Vue 3 Composition API; established Playwright test coverage across all critical flows.
**Relevance:** Direct parallel to Meridian's situation — incomplete vendor handoff, known defects, no tests, operations team blocked. Delivered in 10 weeks.
**Outcome:** IT sign-off within two weeks of test coverage landing. Zero regression incidents in the six months following delivery.

## Engagement 2 — Inventory Forecasting Feature, Tessera Logistics

**Client:** Tessera Logistics (multi-warehouse 3PL operator, 5 facilities across North America)
**Scope:** Designed and built a demand-driven restocking recommendation engine on top of an existing Python/FastAPI backend. Frontend in Vue 3. Recommendations based on stock levels, historical demand, and configurable budget ceilings per facility.
**Relevance:** Direct analogue to R2. The core logic — ranking purchase recommendations against a budget constraint across multiple locations — is the same problem Meridian is asking us to solve.
**Outcome:** Operations team reduced manual reorder work by ~40% in the first quarter of use. Client later requested transactional integration with their procurement system, which we delivered as a follow-on engagement.

## Engagement 3 — Multi-Region i18n Rollout, Koto Electronics

**Client:** Koto Electronics (Japanese manufacturer with European distribution operations)
**Scope:** Extended i18n coverage from English-only to English/Japanese/German across a Vue 3 operations portal. Worked with client-side translators to wire the framework; validated locale correctness with in-country reviewers.
**Relevance:** Direct parallel to D2. Tokyo warehouse staff at Meridian face the same friction Koto's teams faced before this engagement. We know the Vue i18n integration patterns and the process for getting translations reviewed correctly.
**Outcome:** Tokyo team onboarding time dropped from ~3 days to ~4 hours once Japanese-language views were live.
