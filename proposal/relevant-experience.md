**RFP MC-2026-0417 — Inventory Dashboard Modernization**

We have delivered modernization engagements for mid-market distributors and operations-focused organizations across three continents. The engagements below are most directly comparable to Meridian's scope.

## Comparable Engagements

### Halcyon Industrial Supply — Dashboard Remediation & Extension
*Vue 3 / FastAPI / multi-warehouse operations*

Halcyon engaged us after a previous vendor left their inventory dashboard with unresolved defects and no test coverage — a situation directly comparable to Meridian's. We audited the existing codebase, established a Playwright test suite, and resolved fourteen defects in their reporting module over a six-week engagement. We then delivered a new demand forecasting view that their procurement team uses daily. Halcyon IT was able to approve and deploy changes independently within two weeks of engagement close.

---

### Brindlewood Logistics — Inventory System Modernization
*Python backend / Vue frontend / EMEA and APAC operations*

Brindlewood operates warehouses in Rotterdam, Singapore, and Osaka. Their Tokyo-adjacent APAC team had the same i18n gap Meridian describes — English-only views for staff with varying English proficiency. We extended their i18n layer to cover all primary views, coordinated review with their Osaka team leads, and delivered a UI refresh in the same engagement. The Osaka team reported a measurable reduction in data-entry errors within the first month.

---

### Ferrox Components — Legacy Dashboard Migration
*FastAPI / Vue / no prior test coverage*

Ferrox came to us with a Vue 2 / Flask application and no automated tests. We migrated the backend to FastAPI, modernized the frontend to Vue 3 Composition API, and built end-to-end Playwright coverage before touching any production functionality. The migration was completed without a single production incident. Ferrox IT now runs the test suite as a deployment gate.

---

## Why This Engagement Is a Strong Fit

The Meridian engagement combines three things we have done repeatedly: inheriting an incomplete codebase from a previous vendor, establishing test coverage as a precondition for safe change, and delivering new operational capability (restocking, forecasting, demand views) on top of a remediated foundation. We are not learning this pattern on Meridian's budget.
