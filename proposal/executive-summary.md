# Executive Summary

**RFP #MC-2026-0417 — Meridian Components Inventory Dashboard Modernization**
**Submitted by:** [Your Firm Name]
**Date:** April 28, 2026

---

Meridian Components has built a functional inventory dashboard, but an incomplete handoff from the previous vendor has left the operations team working around a broken Reports module, without the restocking tools they need, and without the test coverage that IT requires before approving further changes. The result is a system that works in parts but can't safely move forward.

We understand the priority: unblock operations first, then extend the platform. That means fixing Reports (R1) and establishing test coverage (R3) before layering in new capabilities — not because the timeline demands it, but because R. Tanaka's team needs a system they can trust before they can benefit from one that does more.

Our proposed engagement delivers the four required items in priority order:

- **R1 — Reports remediation.** We will audit the Reports module against the existing codebase, identify all defects, and resolve them. We won't work from an assumed bug count; we'll work until the module is clean.
- **R4 — Architecture documentation.** We'll perform our own review of what the previous vendor built — the handoff notes are a starting point, not a source of truth — and deliver a current-state overview suitable for Meridian IT.
- **R2 — Restocking recommendations.** A new view that surfaces purchase order recommendations given current stock levels, demand forecast data, and an operator-supplied budget ceiling. Designed for the operations team, not just administrators.
- **R3 — Automated browser testing.** End-to-end coverage for all flows touched in this engagement: the remediated Reports module and the new Restocking feature. The goal is a test suite that gives IT a reliable green signal, not checkbox coverage.

The desired items (D1 UI refresh, D2 i18n extension, D3 dark mode) are scoped as a second phase and priced separately, so Meridian can decide how much to pursue after seeing the required work delivered.

We propose a fixed-fee structure for R1–R4 and a time-and-materials option for D1–D3, with a not-to-exceed ceiling. Detailed pricing and phased timeline follow in subsequent sections.
