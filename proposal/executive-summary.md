# Executive Summary

*Accenture response to RFP MC-2026-0417 — Meridian Components, Inc.*

---

We read this as one engagement, not four deliverables. The RFP separates Reports remediation, Restocking, automated testing, and architecture documentation, but the goal underneath is single — restoring Meridian's confidence to evolve a system that today carries unfinished work, no test coverage, and a Reports module that has not behaved as your operations team needs it to. Our proposal is structured around that one goal.

Three principles shape our approach.

**We deliver with your operators, not just for them.** Restocking is your operations team's ask. It is the feature that will most visibly change their day. We treat their input as a delivery dependency, not a wish list — a working session in week 1 calibrates the recommendation algorithm against real lead times, supplier preferences, and stockout tolerance. Our technical approach lays out a reorder-point model with operator-allocated budget logic; week 1 is where we make it Meridian's, not ours.

**Earning your IT team's confidence is the first thing we have to do.** Automated test coverage is listed as one of four required items, but in practice it is the prerequisite that unblocks every change Meridian makes after us. We do not ask IT to define the critical flows for us — we publish the flow list in §3.3 of our technical approach, with coverage at the layers that produce real change-confidence. Week 1 includes an IT calibration step to confirm the list and align on coverage thresholds before remediation work commits.

**Pricing is structured to make delivery predictable.** The previous vendor was, by your read, capable but over-committed. We have sized this engagement to be deliverable, not ambitious — and the commercial structure follows. A single fixed-fee envelope with a Discovery Calibration Clause: week 1 is a discovery sprint that produces a confirmed SOW (Reports defect audit, IT-validated flow list, operator-calibrated R2 scope). From week 2 the fee is committed. If discovery materially changes scope, one change order follows — not a series of revisions.

The work phases into a discovery week followed by parallel delivery of Reports remediation, Restocking, the test harness, and architecture handoff documentation. Phasing and pricing detail follow in the timeline and pricing sections.

At the end of the engagement, Meridian has: a Reports module the operations team trusts, a Restocking capability that changes daily decision-making, a test harness that lets IT approve future changes without hesitation, and architecture documentation any successor — including your own team — can pick up.

— Accenture
