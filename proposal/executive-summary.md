# Executive Summary

**RFP #MC-2026-0417 — Meridian Components Inventory Dashboard Modernization**
**Submitted by:** Accenture&CT Consulting
**Date:** April 28, 2026

---

Meridian Components operates a business-critical inventory dashboard that its operations team relies on every day — and that same dashboard has known defects, no test coverage, and gaps that are actively blocking your Tokyo warehouse staff. The previous vendor left the work unfinished. We are proposing to complete it, stabilize it, and extend it with the capabilities your operations team has been waiting for.

We have reviewed the RFP, the previous vendor's handoff notes, and the source code. Our assessment: the core architecture is sound, but the delivery was incomplete. The Reports module has multiple unresolved defects. There are no automated tests, which is why your IT team cannot safely approve changes. And the Restocking capability your VP of Operations needs does not exist yet.

Our approach addresses this in a deliberate sequence. We will establish automated browser test coverage first — before touching anything else. This unblocks IT, gives your team confidence in every subsequent change, and ensures that the defect fixes and new features we deliver are verifiable. We will then remediate the Reports module, build the Restocking view, and deliver architecture documentation suitable for internal handoff.

We bring direct experience with Vue/Python applications at comparable scale, a clear delivery model, and a team that will not disappear at contract end. Our goal is to leave Meridian with a system your team owns and can maintain — not another handoff document that raises more questions than it answers.

The required scope (R1–R4) is achievable within eight weeks. Desired items (D1–D3) are scoped as optional extensions that can be added within the same engagement or deferred to a follow-on.

We are confident in this scope, this sequence, and this timeline. The detail behind each is in the sections that follow.
