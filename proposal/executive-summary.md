# Executive Summary

**RFP MC-2026-0417 — Inventory Dashboard Modernization**
**Submitted by:** Accenture
**Date:** April 28, 2026

---

Meridian Components' operations team relies on its inventory dashboard every day — and right now, that dashboard is letting them down. The Reports module has known defects that have gone unresolved since the previous vendor's contract ended. There is no automated test coverage, which has led your IT team to block further changes. And the capabilities your operations staff need most — particularly the ability to generate data-driven restocking recommendations — simply don't exist yet.

We understand the core problem: a system that should be a force multiplier for your warehouse teams has instead become a source of friction and uncertainty.

Our approach is straightforward. We will begin with a full audit of the Reports module, discover and resolve every defect, and document our findings. In parallel, we will establish an automated end-to-end test suite — not as a final deliverable, but as the foundation that makes everything else safe to build. With tests in place, we can deliver the Restocking view your operations team has requested and produce architecture documentation that gives your IT team a clear picture of what they're inheriting.

We are proposing a four-week phased engagement: testing and remediation first, new capability second, documentation and handoff third. This sequencing is deliberate — it unblocks your IT team early and reduces risk on the new build.

We have delivered comparable modernization engagements for mid-market distributors operating across multiple time zones and have direct experience with Vue/Python stacks of this type. We know what "limited handoff documentation" means in practice, and we budget for it.

Meridian should expect a vendor who communicates early when something is more complex than scoped, proposes solutions rather than change orders, and leaves you with a codebase your own team can maintain. That is what we are committing to.
